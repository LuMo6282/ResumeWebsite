Endurance Philosophy

Like most from a young age, I was introduced to athletics. However growing up, it's become so much more than working out to me. In moving schools and knowing nobody, I found home in a local crossfit gym. I fell in love with the community values of constant self betterment, consistency, discipline, and commitment to a purpose. These values have grown outside of the gym into mountaineering, endurance racing, and eventually all areas of my life. Fitness will always be a keystone of my life - it being the main source of these values - constantly giving me direction and clarity. I am infinitely grateful for the opportunities I have been given along the way in order to have discovered this passion, as I would not be who I am without it.

Connection to Professional Life

One of the most valuable lessons taught to me comes out of this area of my life - you either did or you didn't. The fact is so many people want to blame their problems and failure on external factors. They make excuses as to why something didn't happen the way they wanted it to. However, in endurance sports, the results are black and white. You either crossed the finish line or you didn't. you either made it to the to the summit or you didn't. There is no in between. This mentality has translated into my professional life as well - from school to personal projects, I hold myself accountable for my actions and results, and I never rely on excuses to explain why something went wrong.
